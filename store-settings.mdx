## **Store Information**

Basic details about your store that customers will see and interact with.

**Store name**\
Your brand name. This shows up on receipts, invoices, and checkout pages.

**Store URL**\
Your public store link.\
This is what customers use to access your store (e.g. `yourstore.blink.store`).

**Support email**\
Where customers can reach you.\
This email appears on receipts and invoices, so make sure it’s active and monitored.

**Location**\
Your business country.\
Used for compliance, payouts, and tax handling.

**Currency**\
Your store’s main currency.\
All prices and sales will be calculated in this currency.

**Supported currencies**

- USD (US Dollar)
- EUR (EURO)
- GBP (British Pound)
- CAD (Canadian Dollar)
- AUD (Australian Dollar)
- JPY (Japanese Yen)
- CHF (Swiss Franc)
- SEK (Swedish Krona)
- INR (Indian Rupee)
- BRL (Brazilian Real)

## **Custom Domain**

Use your own domain instead of your default [yourstore.blink.store](http://yourstore.blink.store) address. Once connected, customers visiting your Blink store will see your custom domain, and anyone visiting your Blink URL will be automatically redirected.

## **Before you start**

You’ll need:

- A domain name you own (for example, `mybrand.com` or `shop.mybrand.com`)
- Access to your domain’s DNS settings through your registrar or hosting provider (such as Cloudflare, GoDaddy, Namecheap, Squarespace, etc.)

## **Step 1: Add your domain**

1. Open **Store Settings → Domains**.
2. Enter your domain name.
3. Click **Add Domain**.

You can connect either:

- A root domain (`yourdomain.com`)
- A subdomain (`store.yourdomain.com`)

After adding it, Blink will generate the DNS records required to verify ownership.

## **Step 2: Configure your DNS**

Blink will display the DNS records you need to add.

For each record:

- **Type** – The DNS record type (A, CNAME, TXT, etc.)
- **Name** – The hostname (often `@` for the root domain)
- **Value** – The destination provided by Blink

Add every record exactly as shown in your domain provider’s DNS settings.

## **Step 3: Verify your domain**

After saving the DNS records:

1. Return to **Store Settings → Domains**.
2. Click **Verify Connection**.

DNS changes usually take a few minutes, but in some cases can take up to **48 hours** to propagate worldwide.

If verification doesn’t succeed immediately, wait a little longer and try again.

## **Domain Statuses**

### **Pending Verification**

Blink is waiting for your DNS records to propagate or match the required configuration.

### **Verified**

Your domain is connected successfully. Customers can now access your store using your custom domain.

Blink will also automatically redirect visitors from your default `yourstore.blink.store` URL to your custom domain.

## **Removing a domain**

Click **Remove** to disconnect your custom domain from Blink.

After removal:

- Your custom domain will no longer point to your Blink store.
- Customers can still access your store using your default `yourstore.blink.store` address.
- You may safely remove the Blink DNS records from your domain provider if you no longer plan to use the domain with Blink.

## **Troubleshooting**

**Verification keeps failing**

- Double-check every DNS record for typos.
- Make sure all required records have been added.
- Wait for DNS propagation if the records were added recently.

**My domain still doesn’t work**

- DNS changes can take up to 48 hours, although they’re often much faster.

**Can I change my custom domain later?**

- Yes. Remove the existing domain and connect a new one at any time.

This style matches Blink’s product voice: concise, friendly, and focused on getting users from point A to point B without overwhelming them with DNS terminology.

## **Refund Policy**

Define how refunds work for your store and what customers should expect.

**Refund policy**\
Choose your refund timeframe (or no refunds).

**Message**\
This is what customers will see before purchasing.\
Use it to clearly explain your policy and avoid confusion later.

<Tip>
  Be clear and direct. A simple, honest policy builds trust and reduces disputes.
</Tip>

## **Search Engine Optimization (SEO)**

Control how your store appears when shared or discovered online.

**SEO Title**\
The headline shown in search results and previews.\
Keep it short and clear (under 60 characters).

**SEO Description**\
A short summary of your store.\
This appears in search engines and link previews.

**SEO Social Image**\
The image shown when your store is shared on platforms like iMessage, Discord, or social media. Use something clean, bold, and on-brand.

## **Tracking & Pixels**

Track performance, conversions, and customer behavior.

**Google Analytics Measurement ID**\
Connect your store to Google Analytics to track traffic and user activity.

**Meta Pixel ID**\
Track conversions from Facebook and Instagram ads.

**X (Twitter) Pixel ID**\
Measure performance from X campaigns.

**TikTok Pixel ID**\
Track conversions and optimize TikTok ads.

**Advanced: Custom Head Scripts**\
Add scripts that load in the `<head>` of your store.\
Useful for analytics tools or third-party integrations.

**Advanced: Custom Body Scripts**\
Add scripts that load before the closing `<body>` tag.\
Typically used for tracking or chat widgets.

Only add scripts if you know what they do — incorrect scripts can break your store.

## **Subscriptions**

Manage how subscriptions and billing are handled.

**Allow multiple subscriptions**\
Customers can have multiple active subscriptions of the same product at the same time.

**Grace period for benefit revocation**\
How long to wait before revoking benefits after a subscription fails all payment retries.

**Customer portal**\
Control what customers can see and do in their portal.

**Allow email address changes**\
Allow customers to change the email address associated with their account.\
\
**Enable subscription plan changes**\
Allow customers to change their subscription plan from the portal.

## **Danger Zone**

This is permanent and cannot be undone.

**Delete store**\
Deletes your store and all associated data.\
Products, customers, sales — everything is removed.

<Note>
  S**tores can only be deleted once all final transactions have fully cleared the holding period and been transferred to your bank account.**
</Note>
